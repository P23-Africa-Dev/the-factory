Weekly CPU Rightsizing (Single Node)
====================================

Use this process while running on a single small node.

1) Collect 5-7 days of steady-state data
-----------------------------------------
- Run:
  - kubectl top nodes
  - kubectl top pods -n factory23
  - ./backend/k8s/rightsize-weekly-check.sh
- Confirm no sustained user-facing latency or login errors during measurement.

2) Decide whether to tune down or up
------------------------------------
- Tune down requests when:
  - pods are stable,
  - node CPU usage remains low,
  - and there are no repeated probe failures or queue lag.
- Tune up requests when:
  - FailedScheduling or probe errors reappear,
  - queue processing slows,
  - or API latency worsens.

3) Change in small increments
-----------------------------
- Adjust cpu requests in 20m to 40m steps only.
- Keep limits unchanged initially; change requests first.
- Deploy and observe for at least 24 hours before the next adjustment.

4) Safety checks after each deploy
----------------------------------
- kubectl get pods -n factory23 -o wide
- kubectl get pods -n ingress-nginx -o wide
- kubectl get endpoints -n ingress-nginx ingress-nginx-controller
- curl https://api.thefactory23.com/api/v1/health

If ingress has zero endpoints, reduce workload pressure immediately
(for example keep backend at 1 replica) until scheduling stabilizes.
